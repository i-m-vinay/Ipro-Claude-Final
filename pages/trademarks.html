The Process

How Trademark Registration Works

India's trademark process has multiple stages. Our team handles every one — keeping you informed at each step.

1

Trademark Search

We search the TM Registry for conflicting marks before filing, reducing your risk of rejection or opposition.

2

Application Filing

We prepare and file the application. You receive the TM acknowledgement number — you can now use the ™ symbol.

3

Examination & Objection

The TM Examiner may raise objections. Our Smart and End to End packages handle all objections and hearings.

4

Registration Certificate

Once approved, your trademark is published and registered. You receive the ® certificate — valid for 10 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about trademark registration in India.

A trademark search is the process of checking existing registered and applied trademarks to ensure your proposed brand name, logo, or tagline is unique and available for registration. You can conduct one on the official IP India website or on Quickcompany Trademark Search.
A proper search helps you:
  • Avoid rejection or objections from the TM Examiner
  • Prevent infringement of existing marks
  • Reduce costly legal disputes
  • Increase chances of successful registration
  • Protect your brand identity from day one
India follows the Nice Classification system with 45 classes: Classes 1–34 cover goods, Classes 35–45 cover services. You must file in the specific class(es) relevant to your business. Filing in the wrong class leaves your brand completely unprotected in your actual market.
We perform:
  • Wordmark search (brand name)
  • Logo/device mark search
  • Phonetic search (similar sounding marks)
  • Class-wise search across relevant categories
  • Owner/Applicant name search
A phonetic search identifies marks that sound similar even if spelled differently — for example, 'Kwik' vs 'Quick'. This is critical because the Trade Marks Act protects against marks that are deceptively similar in sound, not just spelling.
